# UX / Conversion Research Findings — and how v2 maps to them

_Direct web research, June 2026. This grounds the v2 redesign. Each finding is tagged
**[have]** (already in the build), **[add]** (a gap worth closing), or **[note]** (context)._

## Funnel & forms (strongest evidence)

- **Multi-step forms convert far better than long single forms.** Formstack: 13.9% vs 4.5%; multi-step
  cited at ~86% higher; some A/B tests 300%+. **[have]** — our tour/apply funnels are multi-step.
- **Start with a low-friction question; ask contact info LAST.** Open with an easy, benefit-oriented
  choice (e.g. bedrooms), collect name/phone/email only on the final step. **[have]** — beds → timeline → contact.
- **Use image/button selectors instead of text fields** (faster, fewer errors, better on mobile, bigger
  tap targets). **[have]** — choice chips.
- **Show a progress indicator; seed it with visible progress** ("endowed progress effect," +20–30%
  completion). **[have]** — step dots, first dot pre-filled.
- **1–2 questions per step, 2–10 steps total.** **[have]** — 3 steps.
- **Microcopy that reassures** ("no obligation," "takes ~30 seconds," privacy note). **[have]** — added.

## CTAs (Grace Hill, RentVision, multifamily consensus)

- **Specific hard CTAs beat generic.** Use "Schedule a Tour," "See Available Floor Plans,"
  "Book Your Private Tour," "Apply Today" — never "Submit/Click Here/Learn More." **[have]**.
- **Pair a hard CTA with a softer one** to catch browsers who aren't ready (e.g. "View Gallery,"
  "Check Availability," brochure). **[have]** — hard "Schedule a Tour" + soft "Check Live Availability."
- **Place CTAs at logical stopping points** — end of floor plans and amenity sections. **[have]** — CTA bands.
- **Sticky, thumb-friendly mobile CTAs.** **[have]** — sticky mobile Tour/Call/Apply bar.

## High-impact gaps to close (the research says these move the needle)

- **Reviews / ratings / testimonials. [add — top priority]** 84% of residents base leasing decisions on
  online ratings; 71% of renters won't tour a community without strong reviews; 95% read reviews first.
  v2 has no social proof yet. Add a testimonials section + aggregate rating (also feeds the Google Map Pack).
- **Floor-plan walkthrough video. [add]** Narrated floorplan videos drove a cited 145% conversion lift
  (Franklin Group); 93% of marketers report positive video ROI. Add a video slot on floor-plan/home.
- **AI chatbot for 24/7 tour booking & FAQ. [add]** Recommended as a consideration-stage booster.
  Port the v1 scripted+AI chat into v2.
- **Floor-plan detail treatment with live pricing/availability. [add]** Per-plan photos, walkthrough,
  and real-time availability help renters self-qualify before contacting.
- **Timed/exit pop-up offering the tour or the special. [note]** Effective (45–60s trigger) but use
  sparingly and Fair-Housing-neutral; sticky bar is the lighter-touch alternative we already have.

## Design / UI direction (Awwwards real-estate, 2025 trends)

- Patterns that read as premium: **large lifestyle hero imagery, masonry galleries, sticky headers,
  split-screen layouts, smooth scroll/subtle motion, generous whitespace, bento/card systems.**
  **[have]** — hero, masonry gallery, sticky nav, split sections, scroll-reveal, card grids.
- Reference-quality apartment sites cited: **The Beaumont** (2025 Hermes gold), **Vista Denver**
  (hero video + interactive floor-plan tools), **The Oxbow** (minimalist), plus RentCafe award winners.
  Direction confirms our minimalist, imagery-forward approach.

## Net read

v2's **foundation already matches the evidence** (multi-step funnel, specific CTAs, sticky mobile,
schema/geo, honest live-availability links). The biggest remaining conversion levers are **social proof
(reviews/testimonials)**, **video**, and the **chatbot** — that's the priority order for polish.
